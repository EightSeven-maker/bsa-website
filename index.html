01 Who We Are · The Shared Table

A community rooted in
service & belonging

The Breton Sikh Association was founded to create a welcoming home where Sikh traditions meet the vibrant culture of Cape Breton. We believe that seva — selfless service — transcends all boundaries.

From langar events feeding hundreds across Cape Breton to youth mentorship programs empowering the next generation, we are guided by the timeless Sikh principles of equality, compassion, and honest living. We opened our doors so that everyone on the island would have a place at the table.

We do not have a permanent gurdwara yet, but that has not stopped us. We meet in community halls, in homes, and wherever the need is. This is our home for now, and everyone is welcome in it.

02 Our Core Values · Timeless Principles

Three pillars that
guide everything

i

Seva

Rooted in the tradition of selfless service, we serve with open hearts — through langar, outreach, and acts of kindness that uplift every neighbour. Service is not something we schedule; it is how we move through the day.

ii

Equality

We honour the belief that all people are equal regardless of caste, creed, gender, or background. Everyone sits on the same floor. Everyone eats the same food. There is no head of the table here.

iii

Compassion

With daya as our guiding light, we strive to understand, support, and care for every individual. Cape Breton winters are long, so we check in. We drive each other to appointments. We share what we have.

03 Sikh Heritage · The Khalsa Spirit

Rooted in the
Khalsa tradition

The Khalsa — the community of initiated Sikhs — embodies justice, equality, and selfless service. At BSA, these values guide everything we do in Cape Breton.

⚔ The Khanda

Symbol of the Khalsa

The Khanda embodies the four pillars of Sikh sovereignty — spiritual power, temporal authority, the circle of community, and the double-edged sword of discernment.

ੴ Guru Granth Sahib Ji

Our Living Guide

The eternal Guru of the Sikhs — a scripture of universal wisdom, poetry, and love that guides every act of seva and every step toward justice and compassion.

✎ Seva — Selfless Service

Serving with Open Hands

Seva is the heart of Sikh life — giving freely without expectation. From langar to storm relief, we serve Cape Breton the way our faith calls us to.

06 Community Impact · Hurricane Fiona 2022

When Cape Breton needed us —
we showed up

When Hurricane Fiona devastated Cape Breton in September 2022, the Breton Sikh Association answered immediately — cooking meals, delivering clothing, and standing with our neighbours through the storm and beyond.

🍲 Post-Storm Langar

Cooking for Hundreds

BSA volunteers cooked hundreds of free meals for families left without power or food after Fiona. Featured by The PIE News as an example of community strength.

🧥 Winter Clothing Drive

Warmth When It Mattered

Members distributed winter clothing to those in need — covered by SaltWire Atlantic Canada as a powerful example of newcomers giving back to their community.

🏆 Officially Recognised

CBRM Community Award

The Cape Breton Regional Municipality officially recognised BSA for outstanding community service during and after Hurricane Fiona. A proud moment for our sangat.
